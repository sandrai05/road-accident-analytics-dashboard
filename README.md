# Road Accident Analysis Dashboard (2021–2022)

A Power BI dashboard analyzing UK road accident data to identify trends in accident severity, location, and contributing factors, supporting data-driven road safety and policy decisions.

---

## 🚀 Business Problem
Road safety stakeholders require clear visibility into accident patterns to:

- monitor accident frequency and severity
- identify high-risk environments and conditions
- evaluate year-over-year trends
- inform targeted safety interventions

---

## 📊Key Findings

- Total casualties: 195.7K
- Total accidents: 144.4K
- Fatalities: 2,855
- Vehicle impact: Cars account for the majority of casualties (155K+)
- Road type risk: Single carriageways show the highest accident concentration
- Time of occurrence: 74% of accidents occur during daylight
- Location distribution: Urban areas account for 62% of accidents
- Trend insight: Monthly comparisons highlight seasonal variation and shifts between 2021 and 2022

---

## 📈 Dashboard Capabilities
- KPI tracking for accidents, casualties, and severity levels
- Year-over-year comparison (2021 vs 2022)
- Segmentation by:
  - vehicle type
  - road type
  - light conditions
  - urban vs rural distribution
- Geographic mapping of accident locations across the UK
- Monthly trend analysis to identify seasonality

---

## 🧠 Analytical Approach
- Cleaned and structured raw accident data in Excel
- Built a Power BI data model to support time-based and categorical analysis
- Developed DAX measures for:
  - casualty totals
  - severity breakdowns
  - year-over-year comparisons
- Designed dashboard layout to prioritize high-impact safety insights

---

## 📊 Dataset
- UK road accident data (2021–2022)
- Includes accident counts, casualty severity, vehicle types, road conditions, and location data

---

## 🎯 Business Impact

This dashboard enables:

- identification of high-risk road types and environments
- prioritization of safety interventions by location and condition
- monitoring of accident trends over time
- data-driven decision-making for transport and safety planning
